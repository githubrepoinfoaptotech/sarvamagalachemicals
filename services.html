Industries we Serve.

Animal feed:

Magnesium sulphate is used as a dietary supplement for livestock to prevent magnesium deficiency, which can lead to muscle weakness, tremors, and impaired growth.

Food industry:

Magnesium sulphate is used as a firming agent in tofu production, a coagulant in brewing, and a bittering agent in some food products.

Medicine and cosmetics:

Magnesium sulphate is an ingredient in laxatives, bath salts, and some topical ointments.

Textile industry:

Magnesium sulphate is used as a mordant in dyeing processes, helping dyes adhere to fabrics.

Construction materials:

Magnesium sulphate is used as a retardant in the setting of cement, allowing for better control of setting time and improved workability.

Chemical industry:

Magnesium sulphate is used as a catalyst in various chemical reactions and as a desiccant (drying agent) in organic synthesis.

Health and Wellness:

Epsom salt (a form of magnesium sulfate) is widely used in therapeutic baths and as a home remedy for muscle aches, relaxation, and reducing inflammation. It's also used in skincare products for exfoliation and to soothe skin irritations.

Water Treatment Industry

Beyond fertilizers, magnesium sulfate is used in various chemical processes, including the manufacture of other magnesium compounds, as a drying agent in organic synthesis, and in the treatment of industrial wastewater to remove heavy metals.

Yarn Industry:

It's employed in the textile industry for dyeing fabrics, as it assists in enhancing dye absorption and improving color brightness.

Food and Pharmaceuticals:

Magnesium sulfate has applications in food and pharmaceutical industries as a magnesium supplement and as a component in certain medications.

Industrial Use:

It finds use in fireproofing wood and as a coagulant in tofu production. Magnesium sulphate, commonly known as Epsom salt, is widely used in the agriculture industry as a fertilizer. It plays a crucial role in phosphate metabolism, plant respiration, and the activation of enzyme systems1.

Electroplating Industry:

Magnesium sulphate is used as a conductive salt. Adding magnesium sulphate heptahydrate to the nickel plating solution can make the plating solution have better conductivity, making the coating white and soft3.

Environmental Protection Industry:

Magnesium sulphate heptahydrate is used directly as a sewage treatment agent or as a raw material of a sewage treatment agent for industrial sewage treatment3.

Building Materials Industry:

Magnesium sulphate heptahydrate is dissolved in water and reacted with heavy light calcined powder (magnesium oxide) to form magnesium oxysulfide cement3.

Fireproof Materials:

Magnesium sulphate heptahydrate is used as a flame retardant for plastics such as acrylate resins, epoxy resins, unsaturated polyesters, and polyurethanes3.

Industrial Production of Polymer Materials:

Magnesium sulphate monohydrate is used as a polymer emulsion coagulant3. Magnesium sulphate heptahydrate is a component of the spinning bath and an important auxiliary material for viscose fibers and vinylon fibers3.
